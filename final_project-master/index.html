Picasso life

Picasso was a famous spanish painter.Pablo Picasso was born in October 25, 1881, in Malaga. His original name was Pablo Ruiz, He later changed his name to his mother's maiden name Pablo Picasso. Picasso’s Mother was Doña Maria Picasso y Lopez, his father was Don José Ruiz Blasco. His father was an art teacher and painter. As young child Picasso had immense talent for art. Due to this his father began teaching him, as he grew up he spent more time drawing rather than doing school work.

1895, Picasso and his family moved to Barcelona, Spain. He later was accepted to one of the best art schools in the area. He moved to Madrid where the school was located.Due to not licking the strict rules that were placed he began skipping classes.

Once he turned 16 in 1897 he moved back to Barcelona. There he met with other artists in the cafe “ El Quatre Gats “. There he became inspired to attempt different art styles.

Later he moved to Paris, This is where he experienced his blue period. This a period where picasso went through a stage of depression. The period was named this due to the blue that dominated most of his paintings. This period lasted from 1901-1904. In this period he painted scenes of poverty, pain, and isolation.

It still is a powerful anti war painting used to this day.

Picasso continue to create art till his late years. He died on April 8th,1973 at the age of 91. He died in Morgins, France. His art and legacy still live on though and he is still one of the most well known painters to this day.

Michelangelo life

Michelangelo is one of the most famous painters in the renaissance.Michelangelo was born in caprese, Italy 1475. He is known for having amazing art work such as the david and many works within the sistine chapel.

Michelangelo’s family when he was an infant moved to florence, there due to his mother's illness was placed with a family of stonecutters.

Michelangelo never had interest in his father's work so his father agreed to apprentice him to an artist when he was age 13. There michelangelo was introduced to fresco.

After a year at the workshop michelangelo was given the opportunity to work under lorenzo the great, part of the great medici family. Due to this he was able to study many things as well as get connections to florence elite. He was later permitted by the catholic church to study corpses for anatomy.

Michelangelo having done a cupid that was aged. It got sold as an antique, the person having found out eventually let michelangelo keep the money and invited him to rome where he could work for the rest of his life.

After having been commissioned to make the david michelangelo was seen almost as an art celebrity. And he did amazing work.

He was eventually commissioned to decorate the ceiling of the sistine chapel, during this time he fired all his assistants that he did not like and feel were talented enough, he ended up completing the giant thing by himself on October 31,1512.

Though he continues to paint and sculpt he began to get more into architecture. He began to work on julius II tombs and the medici chapel. Later in his life he has problems with his nude images in a holy place, He in general had many fans as well as conflicts.

He died a few weeks before his 89th birthday on February 18th, 1564. He has since then become a huge symbol for art and one of the most respected artists and is known for being a lead in the renaissance.

Raphael was born April 6,1483. He was born in Urbino, Italy. When he was eleven he had to take over his father's workshop. He was said to have surpassed his father's talent, and soon was being commissioned for many things.

From this Raphael was able to go under apprenticeship for 4 years. Hw worked with pietro vannucci.He later moved to florence where he was influenced by the artists he found there. This exposure allowed for him to develop his style with more expression.

Between 1504 - 1507 raphael produced a series of madonnas. This was due to leonardo da vinci and michelangelo influence. Later he was often commissioned to make several paintings within churches, and within the sistine chapel.

Later he was hired as an architect. He did fairly well in this field, having done many religious buildings and even an area in saint peter's basilica. He died in April 6th1520, on his 37th birthday of mysterious causes. His painting and architecture is still regarded as some high work of the renaissance period.
