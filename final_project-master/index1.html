Claude Monet Life

Later monet and his family moved to a town near Normandy. Monet at a young age did not like being confined to a classroom and would often draw. His mother supported his artistic effort while his father wished for him to go into business. After his mother's death Monet grieved immensely.

Monet later became known for drawing towns peoples caricatures. Soon he met Eugene Boudin, a landscape artist in the area. Eugene introduced monet to painting outdoors and landscapes.

In 1859 monet moved to paris to pursue his art. He enrolled as a student in the Academie Suisse.From 1861 to 1862 Monet served in the military. He was dismissed due to health reasons.

Monet enjoyed working outdoors, he painted many things. He was accepted into Salon, two of his paintings were put into an art gallery. Though his painting got extreme praise, he still had financial struggle.

Later the Salon showed another one of monet's works. Camille(women in green), it depicted his lover and later wife Camille Doncieux. She served as his model and would often pose for paintings, she was younger than monet. The often had a hard time with money and extremely struggled when there first son was born, Jean in 1867. This got so bad that monet attempted suicide by drowning himself in the Seine River.
